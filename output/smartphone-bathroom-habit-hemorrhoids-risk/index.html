Scrolling Your Way to Piles? The Hidden Health Cost of Bathroom Smartphone Habits.

👤 K M Manohar | Independent Science and Technology Analyst  |  📅 2026-02-28  | ⏱ 4 min read

The Hidden Health Risk of Using Smartphones in the Bathroom

How a Common Modern Habit Could Be Increasing Your Risk of Hemorrhoids
Smartphones have become inseparable from our daily lives.

Many people carry them everywhere — including the bathroom. What seems like a harmless habit of scrolling through news or social media while sitting on the toilet may actually have unintended health consequences.
Emerging medical research now suggests that smartphone use in the bathroom may significantly increase the risk of hemorrhoids, a painful condition affecting millions worldwide.
The findings highlight how modern digital habits are quietly reshaping even the most basic biological routines.

What the Study Found
Researchers analyzing bathroom habits discovered a striking difference between people who used smartphones on the toilet and those who did not.

Among smartphone users:
37% reported spending more than 5 minutes on the toilet. Among non-smartphone users:
Only 7.1% spent more than 5 minutes
The most common activities during toilet use included:
Browsing online news
Checking social media
  • Messaging or scrolling through apps
The key insight from the study is that smartphone use tends to extend bathroom time unintentionally.
People often lose track of time while scrolling, which leads to prolonged sitting.

Why Sitting Longer Can Cause Hemorrhoids
Hemorrhoids develop when the veins in the rectal and anal region become swollen due to increased
pressure. When someone sits on the toilet for extended periods:
Pressure builds in the rectal veins
Blood circulation in the area slows
Veins begin to swell

Over time, this can lead to painful hemorrhoids, itching, bleeding, and discomfort.
Interestingly, the study also challenged a common assumption.

Previous research often blamed straining during bowel movements as the main cause. However, this study found no strong link between straining and hemorrhoid risk.
Instead, time spent sitting on the toilet appears to be a more important factor. A 46% Higher Risk Linked to Smartphone Use
According to the study’s senior author, gastroenterologist Dr. Trisha Pasricha, people who used smartphones while on the toilet had a 46% higher likelihood of having hemorrhoids.
Her insight highlights a broader issue:

Modern technology is influencing our health in ways we are only beginning to understand.
Smartphones are designed to keep users engaged. Endless scrolling feeds, notifications, and algorithm-driven content make it easy to lose track of time — even during activities meant to take only a few minutes.

A Small Habit With Big Lifestyle Implications
The bathroom smartphone habit may seem trivial, but it reflects a larger shift in modern behavior.

Technology is increasingly invading spaces that were once free from digital distractions — including sleep time, meals, and even bathroom routines.

Health experts warn that such habits can lead to:

Poor digestion patterns
Reduced awareness of natural body signals
Increased sedentary behavior
Lifestyle-related health issues
The bathroom, ideally, should remain a brief and functional activity, not another screen-time session.

Simple Tips to Protect Your Health
Doctors recommend a few simple practices that can significantly reduce the risk of hemorrhoids.
1️⃣ Leave Your Phone Outside
Avoid bringing smartphones into the bathroom. Removing the distraction helps you finish quickly.

2️⃣ Keep Bathroom Time Short
Ideally, bowel movements should take no more than a few minutes.

3️⃣ Improve Digestive Health
A diet rich in fiber, fruits, vegetables, and adequate hydration supports smoother bowel movements.

4️⃣ Stay Physically Active
Regular exercise promotes healthy digestion and prevents constipation.

5️⃣ Pay Attention to Your Body
If bowel movements regularly take longer than usual, it may indicate digestive issues worth discussing with a healthcare professional.

The Bigger Lesson: Mindful Technology Use

Smartphones are powerful tools that improve productivity, communication, and access to information.
However, the key is conscious use.

Small daily habits — even something as routine as bathroom behavior — can influence long-term health outcomes.
Sometimes the healthiest decision is simply knowing when to put the phone down.
Your gut health may depend on it.
