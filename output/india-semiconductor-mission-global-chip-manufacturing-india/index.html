India’s Semiconductor Sunrise: Powering Bharat’s Tech Destiny Under PM Modi

India’s Semiconductor Revolution: How the India Semiconductor Mission Is Building a Global Chip Powerhouse

Introduction: Why Semiconductors Matter More Than Ever

In the modern world, semiconductors have become the foundation of technological civilization. From smartphones and laptops to satellites, electric vehicles, artificial intelligence systems, and defense technologies, nearly every advanced device relies on microchips.

The semiconductor industry is therefore not just an economic sector—it is a strategic pillar of national security and technological independence.

For decades, semiconductor manufacturing has been concentrated in a handful of regions such as Taiwan, South Korea, the United States, and parts of Europe. Countries across the world have realized that relying heavily on external sources for chips creates vulnerabilities, especially during geopolitical tensions or supply chain disruptions.

India, with its rapidly expanding digital economy and massive electronics consumption, has historically imported the majority of its semiconductor needs. Recognizing the urgency of building domestic capabilities, the Government of India launched an ambitious initiative called the India Semiconductor Mission (ISM).

With investments worth ₹1.60 lakh crore and multiple large-scale projects across the country, India is taking decisive steps to become a major global semiconductor hub.

The Global Semiconductor Landscape

To understand the significance of India’s semiconductor mission, it is important to first look at the global semiconductor ecosystem.

The semiconductor industry is one of the most complex manufacturing sectors in the world. A typical chip involves multiple stages, including:

• Research and development
• Design and architecture
• Fabrication in semiconductor fabs
• Assembly and packaging
• Testing and quality control

A single semiconductor chip can pass through dozens of countries and hundreds of suppliers before reaching the final product.

For years, a small number of companies have dominated chip manufacturing. Taiwan’s semiconductor industry, for example, produces a large share of the world’s advanced chips. South Korea and the United States also play critical roles in design and fabrication.

However, global supply chains were severely disrupted during the COVID-19 pandemic, which caused chip shortages affecting industries ranging from automobiles to consumer electronics.

This crisis forced governments worldwide to reconsider their semiconductor strategies.

India’s Strategic Response: The India Semiconductor Mission

India’s response to the semiconductor challenge came in the form of the India Semiconductor Mission, launched as part of the broader vision of technological self-reliance and digital transformation.

The mission was designed to build an integrated semiconductor ecosystem that covers the entire value chain.

Core Objectives of the Mission

The India Semiconductor Mission focuses on several key goals:

• Establishing semiconductor fabrication plants (fabs) within India
• Developing assembly, testing, marking, and packaging facilities (ATMP)
• Supporting semiconductor design startups
• Encouraging advanced research in chip technologies
• Creating a strong supply chain for electronics manufacturing

The program also provides financial incentives, infrastructure support, and policy stability to attract both domestic and international semiconductor companies.

Major Investments and Projects

India’s semiconductor strategy is backed by significant financial commitments and policy incentives.

So far, 10 major semiconductor and electronics manufacturing projects have been approved across six states, representing investments worth ₹1.60 lakh crore.

These projects include semiconductor fabrication plants, chip packaging units, and advanced electronics manufacturing facilities.

The scale of investment signals that India is not merely experimenting with semiconductor manufacturing—it is committing to long-term industrial transformation.

Emerging Semiconductor Hubs Across India

One of the defining features of India’s semiconductor mission is its geographical diversification. Instead of concentrating the industry in a single location, the government is developing multiple semiconductor clusters across the country.

Dholera, Gujarat

Dholera is emerging as India’s flagship semiconductor manufacturing hub. Located within the Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor, Dholera offers advanced infrastructure designed to support high-tech manufacturing industries.

The region is expected to host semiconductor fabrication facilities capable of producing chips for a wide range of applications.

Morigaon, Assam

The northeastern state of Assam has also entered India’s semiconductor ecosystem with projects in Morigaon. These facilities focus on assembly, testing, marking, and packaging operations, which are critical steps in semiconductor manufacturing.

This initiative represents a significant step toward balanced regional industrial development.

Other Participating States

Additional semiconductor-related investments are being developed in states including:

• Karnataka
• Tamil Nadu
• Telangana
• Uttar Pradesh

These states already host strong electronics and technology ecosystems, making them ideal locations for semiconductor-related industries.

India’s Competitive Advantage: Design Talent

Although India is relatively new to semiconductor manufacturing, the country already holds a strong position in semiconductor design.

India is home to approximately 20 percent of the world’s semiconductor design engineers.

Many leading global chip companies operate large design centers in Indian cities such as:

• Bengaluru
• Hyderabad
• Chennai
• Noida
• Pune

These engineers work on designing complex microchips used in smartphones, automotive systems, and data centers.

The India Semiconductor Mission aims to leverage this existing strength and expand India’s role from design to full-scale manufacturing.

Economic Impact and Job Creation

The semiconductor mission is expected to generate significant economic and employment opportunities.

High-Value Job Creation

Semiconductor manufacturing requires highly skilled professionals, including:

• electrical engineers
• materials scientists
• process engineers
• chip designers
• data scientists

Thousands of direct jobs are expected to be created in semiconductor facilities.

In addition, indirect employment opportunities will emerge in areas such as:

• logistics
• equipment manufacturing
• construction
• maintenance services
• research and development

The overall employment impact could reach hundreds of thousands of jobs over the coming decade.

Boost to the Electronics Manufacturing Industry

India is already one of the world’s largest consumers of electronics products. Domestic semiconductor manufacturing will strengthen industries such as:

• smartphones
• consumer electronics
• electric vehicles
• telecommunications equipment
• medical electronics

This will help India reduce import dependence and build a self-reliant electronics manufacturing ecosystem.

Strategic Importance for National Security

Semiconductors are not only important for consumer technologies but also for national defense and strategic industries.

Modern military systems depend heavily on advanced microchips for:

• radar systems
• missile guidance
• satellite communications
• cyber defense
• artificial intelligence systems

Developing domestic semiconductor capabilities enhances India’s technological sovereignty and national security.

Global Supply Chain Realignment

The global semiconductor industry is undergoing a significant transformation as countries seek to diversify supply chains.

Governments in the United States, Japan, and Europe have launched major semiconductor programs to strengthen domestic manufacturing.

India’s semiconductor mission aligns with this broader trend, offering global companies an opportunity to establish trusted manufacturing partnerships in a rapidly growing market.

India’s advantages include:

• a large and skilled workforce
• strong policy support
• a growing domestic market
• improving infrastructure
• geopolitical stability

These factors position India as a promising destination for semiconductor investments.
