India Lays Foundation for Its First Drone City & Space Cities — A Bold Leap into the Future

👤 K M Manohar | Independent Science and Technology Analyst  |  📅 2026-03-01  | ⏱ 7 min read

 India Lays Foundation for Its First Drone City & Space Cities — A Bold Leap into the Future

India has taken a historic step toward becoming a global technology powerhouse with the announcement of the country’s first Drone City and twin Space Cities in Andhra Pradesh.

The foundation stone was virtually laid during the 30th CII Partnership Summit in Visakhapatnam, marking a major milestone in India’s aerospace and emerging technology ecosystem.

The initiative was unveiled by N. Chandrababu Naidu along with Piyush Goyal, signaling strong collaboration between the state government and the central government.

These ambitious projects aim to position India at the forefront of drone technology, private space innovation, advanced manufacturing, and high-skill employment generation.

More importantly, they represent a strategic shift in India’s development model—from traditional infrastructure expansion to future-focused technology ecosystems.

A Vision That Looks Decades Ahead

The announcement reflects a broader vision to transform India into a global hub for advanced technologies.

Chief Minister Naidu described the moment as “historic”, emphasizing that the projects are not merely infrastructure developments but platforms for building the next generation of technological leadership.

The government’s vision includes:

  • Drone taxis within the next two years

  • Development of a dedicated drone traffic control system

  • Large-scale training of drone pilots

  • Creation of a private space manufacturing ecosystem

  • Integration of AI, robotics, satellite technology, and advanced materials

Such initiatives are expected to push India into the frontline of global aerospace innovation, a sector projected to grow dramatically over the coming decades.

Drone City in Orvakal: Building India’s Drone Capital


One of the most ambitious components of the initiative is India’s first Drone City, which will be developed on 300 acres in Orvakal, Kurnool district.

The project is designed as a comprehensive drone ecosystem, integrating research, manufacturing, training, and commercial services under a single platform.

Core Objectives of Drone City

The goal is to transform India into a global drone innovation hub, capable of serving both domestic and international markets.

The ecosystem will support:

  • Drone design and prototyping

  • Manufacturing of commercial and industrial drones

  • Drone testing corridors

  • R&D laboratories

  • Training centers for remote pilots

  • Startup incubation and innovation hubs

According to government projections, the project could generate over 40,000 jobs, both directly and indirectly.

In addition, the state plans to train 25,000 remote drone pilots, addressing a major skill gap in the rapidly expanding drone industry.

Attractive Incentives to Build a Global Ecosystem

To attract global players and startups, the Andhra Pradesh government has introduced aggressive investment incentives.

These include:

  • 20% capital subsidy for eligible companies

  • 100% SGST reimbursement

  • Infrastructure support

  • Dedicated testing airspace

  • Policy support for startups

Several companies have already signed Memorandums of Understanding (MoUs), including:

  • Algo Botics

  • Akin Analytics Solution

  • Sense Image

  • Aerospace Industries

These partnerships indicate that the Drone City will operate not just as a manufacturing zone, but as a collaborative innovation cluster connecting startups, universities, research institutions, and industry leaders.

Why the Drone Industry Matters

The global drone market is expected to grow dramatically over the next decade.

According to industry estimates, the global drone economy could exceed $100 billion by 2030.

Drones are transforming multiple sectors:

Agriculture

Precision agriculture drones can monitor crops, detect pests, and optimize fertilizer use.

Infrastructure Inspection

Drones can inspect bridges, power lines, pipelines, and construction sites faster and more safely than traditional methods.

Logistics

Drone deliveries are expected to revolutionize last-mile logistics, especially in rural and remote areas.

Disaster Management

Drones can quickly survey disaster zones, deliver emergency supplies, and assist in rescue operations.

Defense and Security

Autonomous drones are increasingly used for surveillance and tactical operations.

India’s Drone City initiative aims to capture a significant share of this rapidly expanding market.


Alongside Drone City, Andhra Pradesh has proposed the development of two dedicated Space Cities in:

  • Sri Sathya Sai district

  • Tirupati district

These projects aim to support the rapid growth of India’s commercial space sector, which has seen significant reforms in recent years.

The creation of private-sector-friendly policies has already begun transforming India’s space ecosystem.

What the Space Cities Will Do

The twin Space Cities are envisioned as end-to-end space industry hubs.

They will support:

Satellite Prototyping

Startups and companies will be able to design and test satellite technologies.

Component Manufacturing

Production of key satellite components such as sensors, propulsion systems, and electronics.

Startup Incubation

Support for emerging space startups through funding, mentorship, and infrastructure.

Launch Logistics

Facilities for mission planning, ground support, and payload preparation.

These capabilities are essential for building a self-reliant space industry.

The Rise of India’s Private Space Sector

For decades, India’s space program was dominated by the Indian Space Research Organisation.

However, policy reforms in recent years have opened the sector to private participation.

This has led to the emergence of several innovative space startups, including:

  • Skyroot Aerospace

  • Agnikul Cosmos

  • Pixxel

  • Bellatrix Aerospace

The government has also created IN-SPACe, which acts as a regulatory and promotional body for private space activities.

The upcoming Space Cities are expected to further accelerate this transformation.

Investment Potential: ₹25,000 Crore Opportunity

The combined projects are expected to attract ₹25,000 crore in investments over the next decade.

In addition, they are projected to create around 35,000 jobs across various sectors.

These jobs will span multiple skill categories:

  • Aerospace engineers

  • Robotics specialists

  • Drone operators

  • AI engineers

  • Satellite designers

  • Manufacturing technicians

  • Data scientists

This will help create a high-skill workforce aligned with future technology industries.

Strategic Partnerships Already Underway

The state government has already signed collaboration agreements with companies such as:

  • Blue Space

  • Eternal Exploration Guild

These partnerships will support technology development, research collaboration, and global integration.

By working with international partners, India aims to integrate its aerospace ecosystem with global supply chains.


One of the most futuristic elements of the announcement is the plan to create a dedicated drone traffic management system.

As drone usage increases, managing low-altitude airspace will become critical.

The proposed system will function similarly to traditional air traffic control, but designed specifically for autonomous aerial vehicles.

This will enable:

  • Safe drone deliveries

  • Urban drone taxis

  • Emergency response drones

  • Infrastructure monitoring drones

If implemented successfully, India could become one of the first countries to build a national drone mobility network.

Quantum Valley: The Next Technological Frontier

Adding further momentum to Andhra Pradesh’s technology ambitions, Chief Minister Naidu also revealed plans to establish Quantum Valley in Amaravati by January 2026.

This futuristic hub will focus on:

  • Quantum computing

  • Artificial intelligence

  • Advanced materials

  • Green hydrogen technologies

The initiative aims to create a deep-tech innovation ecosystem similar to Silicon Valley or Israel’s startup clusters.

Together with Drone City and Space Cities, Quantum Valley will help Andhra Pradesh evolve into a global technology innovation hub.

Why Andhra Pradesh Is Positioning Itself as India’s Tech Gateway

Andhra Pradesh’s strategy focuses on early entry into emerging technologies.

By investing in sectors such as drones, space technology, AI, and quantum computing, the state aims to build a long-term competitive advantage.

This aligns with the state’s ambitious goal of attracting $1 trillion in investments over the next decade.

The approach focuses on:

  • High-technology industries

  • Innovation ecosystems

  • Skilled workforce development

  • Global partnerships

If successful, Andhra Pradesh could become one of India’s most advanced technology corridors.

The Global Race for Aerospace Leadership

Countries around the world are investing heavily in aerospace innovation.

Major global players include:

  • The United States

  • China

  • Israel

  • European Union nations

India’s initiatives indicate that the country intends to compete aggressively in this domain.

By combining government support, private innovation, and research collaboration, India aims to build a globally competitive aerospace ecosystem.

A New Era for India’s Technology Economy

The launch of Drone City, Space Cities, and Quantum Valley represents more than a set of infrastructure projects.

It marks the beginning of a new innovation-driven economic strategy.

These initiatives could:

  • Generate tens of thousands of high-skill jobs

  • Attract billions in investment

  • Position India as a global technology leader

  • Accelerate the growth of startups and research institutions

If executed effectively, Andhra Pradesh’s vision may become a blueprint for India’s next technological revolution.

The foundation stones laid today could very well shape the industries, innovations, and economic landscape of India for decades to come.
